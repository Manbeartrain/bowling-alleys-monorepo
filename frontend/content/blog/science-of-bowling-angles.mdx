## What is Entry Angle?

The entry angle is the path your bowling ball takes as it approaches the pins, specifically **the angle at which it enters the pocket** (the space between the 1 and 3 pins for right-handers, or the 1 and 2 pins for left-handers).

- A ball that comes in too straight often results in **corner pins left standing** (like the dreaded 10-pin).
- A ball that hooks too sharply can cause **splits**.
- The “sweet spot” is typically **about 4–6 degrees of entry angle**, which maximizes strike percentage.

---

## Why Does It Matter?

Bowling is all about **pin action**. When your ball enters the pocket at the right angle:

- The head pin deflects naturally into the 2-pin (for right-handers).
- The ball continues through the 3-pin and into the 5-pin.
- Energy transfers across the pin deck, creating the **domino effect** that clears the rack.

Miss the angle, and you’ll see more spares instead of strikes—even if you hit the pocket.

---

## How to Control Your Entry Angle

Here are a few ways to improve your entry angle on the lanes:

1. **Ball Speed** – Slower speeds give your ball more time to hook into the pocket.  
2. **Axis Rotation** – Adjust your release; more side roll equals a sharper angle.  
3. **Lane Conditions** – Oily lanes reduce hook, dry lanes increase it. Pay attention and adapt.  
4. **Starting Position** – Move your feet left or right on the approach to fine-tune where your ball hits the pocket.
