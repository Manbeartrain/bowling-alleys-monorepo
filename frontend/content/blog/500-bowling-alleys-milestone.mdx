## What We've Learned

Building a directory of 500+ bowling alleys taught us some fascinating things about America's bowling scene:

### 1. Bowling Alleys Are More Diverse Than You Think

Every region has its own bowling culture. New England has candlepin and duckpin bowling that you won't find anywhere else. The Midwest has historic bowling centers that have served their communities for 70+ years. The West Coast has trendy "boutique bowling" experiences with full restaurants and bars. The South has massive family entertainment centers where bowling is just one of a dozen activities.

There's no single "bowling alley experience"—and that's what makes this industry so interesting to document.

### 2. Pricing Varies Wildly (And Not How You'd Expect)

You'd assume that bowling in expensive cities costs more. Sometimes that's true—but we've found $8/game alleys in San Francisco and $25/hour alleys in rural towns with resort-style facilities.

The biggest factor isn't location, it's business model. Traditional bowling alleys keep prices low and make money on volume and food sales. Entertainment centers charge premium prices for the full "experience." Boutique alleys target date nights and adults-only crowds with craft beer and upscale pricing.

Knowing what you're paying before you arrive is exactly why we built this platform.

### 3. Bowling Alley Owners Care (A Lot)

Since launching, we've heard from dozens of bowling alley owners who want their information to be accurate and helpful. They've corrected hours, updated amenities, and shared photos. Some have claimed their listings and are actively managing their profiles.

This told us something important: there's a real need for a platform that gives bowling alleys visibility while helping bowlers find the perfect place to play.
